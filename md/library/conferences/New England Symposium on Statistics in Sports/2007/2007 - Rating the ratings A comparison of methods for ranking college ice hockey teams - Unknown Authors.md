## ‚ÄĘ **The approach:** 

- Use Monte Carlo simulation of many seasons to compare the effectiveness of different ratings methods to rank order teams with assumed ‚Äúknown‚ÄĚ abilities. 

## **<u>WPct: Raw Winning Percentage</u>** 

- ‚ÄĘTies count as ¬Ĺ win 

## **RPIw: Ratings Percentage Index**<sup>**1**</sup> 

- ‚ÄĘAdjust for strength of opponents 

_RPIw_ = 3.0* _WPct_ + .024* _AvgOppWpct_ + .046* _OppOppWPct_ Or other weights, e.g. 25%, 21%, 54% for _RPIm_ 

## **KRACH: Ken‚Äôs Rating for American College Hockey**<sup>**2**</sup> 

‚ÄĘRecursively-defined Bradley-Terry model _KRACH_ = _KYou_ = ‚ąĎ _WinRatio_ 1 ‚ąó _Games_ ‚ąĎ _KYouK_ + _OppKOpp Games KYou_ + _KOpp_ Standardize so that WPct=0.500 ÔŅĹ Krach=100 

### **The CHODR**<sup>**3**</sup> **simulation method:** 

When Team A plays Team B: 

Goals for A ~ Poisson(őĽAB) 

> where őĽ _AB_ = _Off_ _<u>A</u>_ √ó _DefB_ ‚čÖ _H_ ¬Ī 1 ¬Ķ 

¬Ķ = average scoring rate,  H = home ice advantage 

### **The ‚Äútrue‚ÄĚ rankings:** 

Assume a balanced schedule among all teams and use the CHODR Poisson scoring rates to compute a probability of winning vs. every other team.  The **expected winning percentage** is 

‚ąĎ _P_ ( _Win_ ) + <u>12</u> _P_ ( _Tie_ ) _EWPct_ = _AllOpponen ts_ # _Opponents_
